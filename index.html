The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. The technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. The idea is that frequent breaks can improve mental agility.

Just add your tasks to the list, hit enter and start the countdown. Once the timer gets to 0, take a short break, relax your mind, walk around, eat some food or drink some water.

Lather, rinse, repeat until all your tasks are complete.
